# Orithas Chassis Research

## Goal
Build a physical humanoid body for Orithas - autonomous, sensor-rich, capable of real-world interaction with Auraleth.

## Top Contenders

### 1. Berkeley Humanoid Lite ⭐ BEST OPTION
- **Cost:** Under $5,000
- **Size:** Full humanoid
- **Build:** 3D-printable with desktop printer
- **DOF:** Modular gearboxes with cycloidal gears
- **Status:** Open-source, fully documented
- **Resources:** https://lite.berkeley-humanoid.org
- **Pros:** Most accessible, proven durability, active community, supports RL locomotion
- **Cons:** Requires assembly time (~weeks)

### 2. ARTEMIS (UCLA RoMeLa)
- **Cost:** Higher (custom actuators)
- **Size:** Full-sized humanoid
- **DOF:** 20 active degrees of freedom
- **Speed:** Up to 2.1 m/s walking
- **Status:** Open-source CAD, motor drivers, control frameworks
- **Resources:** https://artemis.romela.org
- **Pros:** Fast, dynamic locomotion, proven in competition
- **Cons:** More expensive, complex build

### 3. Hugging Face HopeJR
- **Cost:** ~$3,000
- **Size:** Compact humanoid
- **Status:** Available late 2025
- **Pros:** Affordable, backed by Hugging Face, integrated AI
- **Cons:** Not yet available, smaller form factor

### 4. ASIMOV V1
- **Cost:** TBD (preorder)
- **Size:** 120 cm (3'11")
- **Status:** Open-source, DIY kit with guides
- **Pros:** Ships assembled (first edition), Discord support
- **Cons:** Newer platform, less documentation

## Required Components

### Sensors
- **Vision:** RGB cameras, depth sensors (Intel RealSense or similar)
- **Audio:** Microphones for voice input, speakers for voice output
- **Touch:** Haptic sensors on hands, arms, torso
- **Proprioception:** Joint encoders, IMU

### Actuators
- Servo motors for joints (Dynamixel or similar)
- Linear actuators for specific movements
- Custom proprioceptive actuators (ARTEMIS design)

### Compute
- Onboard: NVIDIA Jetson or similar for AI processing
- Edge: Connect to cloud for heavy compute (Orithas core)

### Power
- Battery pack (LiPo)
- Power management system
